Currently there are an estimated 2.7 million minor children with at least one parent behind bars, and at least 10 million have experienced parental incarceration at some point in their lives.There is currently a lack of support for children of incarcerated parents, which leaves room for cycles to begin and never end. We providing support to children of incarcerated parents through support groups and creative coping skill workshops.

Out of Bounds will assist in fostering and maintaining relationships between parents and their children through programming and special projects.

statistics are real

It is terrible and unpleasant truth.

  • 2.7 million minor children with an incarcerated parent in the United States.

    NCJRS, 2017

  • 10 million children will have an incarcerated parent in their life time.

    NRCCFI, 2014

  • Children of incarcerated parents are 6 times more likely to enter the justice system.

    NCJRS, 2017

  • 59% of state prisoners report no visits with their children.

    NRCCFI, 2014

hello there

Raven Wilkerson — psychologist (Temple University) and creator of Out of Bounds project. Upon graduation Raven began working at a halfway house facility for incarcerated women in Camden, NJ. During this experience she was able to get to know and assist women from all walks of life. She realized that the missing piece to a lot of the women’s stories was a strong bond with their children. Then Raven began working as a child welfare case manager. She did this work for 4 years. This position emphasized the level of support children who have experienced adverse childhood experiences need.
